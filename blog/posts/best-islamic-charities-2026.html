Choosing the right organization for your Zakat is just as important as calculating the correct amount. Your Zakat must reach eligible recipients as defined by Islamic law, and reputable charities ensure proper distribution according to Sharia guidelines.

We have researched and compiled a list of the most trusted Islamic charities accepting Zakat in 2026. Each organization has been evaluated based on their transparency, Sharia compliance, global reach, and track record of impact.

What to Look for in a Zakat-Eligible Charity

Before we review individual organizations, here are the key factors to consider when choosing where to pay your Zakat:

  • Sharia compliance — The organization should have Islamic scholars overseeing Zakat collection and distribution to ensure it reaches the eight eligible categories mentioned in the Quran.
  • Transparency — Look for published annual reports, financial audits, and clear breakdowns of how funds are allocated.
  • Zakat-specific programs — Some charities pool Zakat with general donations. Ideally, Zakat funds should be kept separate and distributed only to eligible recipients.
  • Track record — Established organizations with years of experience are generally more reliable in their distribution networks.
  • Overhead transparency — Understand what percentage of your donation goes directly to recipients versus administrative costs.

Who Can Receive Zakat?

According to the Quran (9:60), there are eight categories of people eligible to receive Zakat:

  1. Al-Fuqara (The Poor) — Those who do not have enough to meet basic needs.
  2. Al-Masakin (The Needy) — Those who have some income but it is insufficient.
  3. Amil Zakat (Zakat Administrators) — Those employed to collect and distribute Zakat.
  4. Mu'allafat al-Qulub (Reconciliation of Hearts) — New Muslims or those sympathetic to Islam.
  5. Fir-Riqab (Freeing Captives) — Freeing those in bondage or slavery.
  6. Al-Gharimin (Those in Debt) — Those burdened by overwhelming debt.
  7. Fi Sabilillah (In the Cause of Allah) — Those striving in the path of Allah.
  8. Ibn as-Sabil (The Wayfarer) — Stranded travelers who need assistance.

Reputable charities ensure your Zakat reaches people in one or more of these categories. When choosing an organization, you can often specify which category you would like your Zakat directed toward.

Tips for Giving Zakat to Charities

  • Specify "Zakat" when donating — Make sure you select the Zakat fund specifically, not a general donation fund.
  • Ask for a receipt — Zakat donations to registered charities are often tax-deductible. Keep receipts for your tax records.
  • Verify Sharia compliance — Ensure the organization has an Islamic advisory board overseeing Zakat distribution.
  • Diversify your giving — Consider splitting your Zakat between multiple organizations to maximize impact across different causes.
  • Give locally too — While international organizations are important, your local mosque or community may also distribute Zakat to needy families in your area.

Can I Give Zakat Directly to Individuals?

Yes, you may give Zakat directly to eligible individuals rather than through an organization. In fact, giving directly to someone you know is in need can be a deeply personal and impactful way to fulfill your obligation. The key requirement is that the recipient must fall into one of the eight categories mentioned above.

However, if you are unsure whether someone qualifies, or if you want to ensure systematic distribution to those most in need, giving through an established charity provides peace of mind that your Zakat is reaching the right people.

Conclusion
