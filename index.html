What is Zakat?

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam — an obligatory annual charity paid by eligible Muslims on their wealth. The word Zakat means purification and growth in Arabic. By paying Zakat, Muslims purify their wealth and fulfill a religious duty to support those in need.

The Quran mentions Zakat alongside Salah (prayer) over 30 times, highlighting its importance. Zakat is 2.5% of your total zakatable wealth that has been held for one full lunar year (Hawl) above the Nisab threshold.

Who Must Pay Zakat?

  • Adult Muslim (reached puberty)
  • Sane and of sound mind
  • Owns wealth above the Nisab threshold
  • Wealth held for one full lunar year
  • Free from debt that would reduce wealth below Nisab

Who Receives Zakat?

  • The poor (Al-Fuqara)
  • The needy (Al-Masakeen)
  • Zakat administrators
  • New Muslims and those whose hearts need support
  • Those in debt
  • Travellers in need
  • Those serving in the way of Allah

Nisab Threshold 2026 — Gold & Silver Standard

The Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before Zakat becomes obligatory. There are two standards used by scholars:

🏭 Gold Nisab (Recommended)

Based on 87.48 grams of gold (7.5 tola). This is the standard used by most major Islamic institutions worldwide including Islamic Relief, National Zakat Foundation, and ISNA.

How to Calculate Zakat on Gold, Savings & Cash

Zakat is 2.5% of your total net zakatable wealth above the Nisab, held for one lunar year. Here's what counts:

💰 Zakat on Savings & Cash

All cash in hand, bank accounts (savings, current, fixed deposits) is fully zakatable. Add up all your liquid cash and bank balances. If the total exceeds Nisab, pay 2.5%.

Example: $10,000 savings × 2.5% = $250 Zakat

🏭 Zakat on Gold

Gold jewellery, coins, and bars are zakatable at market value. Most scholars exclude jewellery worn daily, but this is debated. Gold held as investment is always zakatable.

Example: 100g gold at $90/g = $9,000 × 2.5% = $225 Zakat

📈 Zakat on Investments

Stocks, mutual funds, and business inventory are zakatable on their current market value. Pay Zakat on the zakatable portion of your investment portfolio annually.

Example: $20,000 investments × 2.5% = $500 Zakat
